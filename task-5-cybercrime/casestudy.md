## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where an attacker sends a fake message that appears to come from a trusted source. The goal is to trick the recipient into sharing sensitive information such as login credentials, banking details, or personal data.

---

## How It Happens – Step by Step

1. The attacker creates a fake email using a slightly altered version of a real company's email address (e.g., hr.infosys.careers@gmail.com instead of an official domain).
2. The email includes a professional logo, a convincing subject line like "Urgent: Internship Selection – Immediate Action Required", and a link to a fake form.
3. The message creates urgency — "respond within 12 hours or your slot will be cancelled."
4. The victim, excited about the opportunity, clicks the link without verifying the sender.
5. The fake form asks for name, academic details, and email login credentials "for verification."
6. The victim submits the form, unknowingly handing over their credentials.
7. The attacker uses the credentials to access the victim's accounts within hours.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- College students actively applying for internships and jobs
- Young professionals looking for early career opportunities
- People who respond quickly to messages without verifying sources
- Anyone in a moment of excitement, fear, or urgency

---

## Consequences

- Unauthorised access to email and linked accounts
- Potential identity theft and misuse of personal data
- Financial loss if banking apps are linked to compromised accounts
- Significant anxiety, loss of trust in online platforms
- Difficulty recovering accounts if two-factor authentication was not enabled
