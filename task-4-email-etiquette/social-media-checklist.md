# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Prepared by:** Khush M Lohar | 25BAI11336
**Course:** CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | VIT Bhopal University

---

## DO's

1. **Do keep your profiles professional.** Think of LinkedIn and GitHub as your digital resume — post only content you would be comfortable showing a recruiter or professor.

2. **Do verify information before sharing.** Before forwarding news, results, or announcements, check whether the source is official and reliable. Spreading misinformation, even unintentionally, damages your credibility.

3. **Do use privacy settings actively.** Review who can see your posts, location, and personal information on each platform. Update these settings regularly.

4. **Do credit others for their work.** If you share someone else's content, code, design, or idea, always mention the original creator.

5. **Do think before you post.** Ask yourself — would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this? If not, do not post it.

---

## DON'Ts

1. **Don't post in anger or frustration.** Venting about teachers, classmates, or institutions publicly can have lasting consequences. What feels satisfying in the moment can permanently damage relationships and reputation.

2. **Don't share personal information publicly.** Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID number, or daily schedule on public platforms.

3. **Don't exaggerate or misrepresent your skills and achievements.** Listing skills you do not have or claiming credit for work you did not do may seem harmless but can backfire during interviews or in professional settings.

4. **Don't engage with or spread unverified rumours.** Forwarding fake news, exam leaks, or unverified college announcements creates panic and misinformation.

5. **Don't use casual or abusive language in professional spaces.** Comments, replies, and posts on LinkedIn, GitHub, or Stack Overflow are visible to the professional community. Maintain the same tone you would use in a formal email.

---

*Your digital footprint is permanent. Build it with care.*
